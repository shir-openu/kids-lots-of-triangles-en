Exercise 1 - Part A
Given: ∠A₁ = ∠A₂ = ∠A₃ = 20°
∠CDE = 75° (exterior angle)
Use the data in the diagram to calculate the angles:
A. Calculate:
∠ADE = ?
Hint: ∠ADE and ∠CDE are supplementary angles on a line. Their sum is 180°
Correct!
Solution:
∠ADE + ∠CDE = 180° (supplementary angles)
∠ADE + 75° = 180°
∠ADE = 105°
Exercise 1 - Part B
Given: ∠CAD = 20° (the angle marked "1")
∠CDE = 75° exterior angle of △ACD
B. Calculate:
∠ACD = ?
Hint: Exterior angle theorem - exterior angle = sum of 2 non-adjacent interior angles
Excellent!
Exterior Angle Theorem:
∠CDE = ∠CAD + ∠ACD
75° = 20° + ∠ACD
∠ACD = 55°
Exercise 1 - Part C
Known: ∠BAC = 60° (three times 20°)
∠ACB = 55°
C. Calculate:
∠ABC = ?
Hint: Sum of angles in a triangle = 180°
Great!
Sum of angles in a triangle:
∠BAC + ∠ABC + ∠ACB = 180°
60° + ∠ABC + 55° = 180°
∠ABC = 65°
Exercise 2 - Part A

Hint: AD bisects angle A

Excellent!

∠ABD + 25° + 110° = 180°
∠ABE = 60°

Exercise 3 - Isosceles Triangle - Part A
Given: △ABC is an isosceles triangle (AB = AC)
The base angle of the triangle is 64°
AK bisects ∠DAC
A. Calculate all the angles - what is the apex angle?
∠BAC = ?
Hint: Sum of angles in a triangle = 180°
Correct!
∠BAC + 64° + 64° = 180°
∠BAC = 180° - 128° = 52°
Exercise 3 - Part B - Proof
Known:
∠DAC = 180° - 52° = 128°
∠KAC = 128° ÷ 2 = 64° (AK bisects)
∠ACB = 64°
B. Why is BC || AK?
Hint: ∠KAC and ∠ACB are on opposite sides of AC
Excellent!
∠KAC = ∠ACB = 64°
These are equal alternate angles relative to transversal AC.
Therefore: BC || AK
